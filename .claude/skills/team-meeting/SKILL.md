# Virtual Lab Team Meeting

This skill orchestrates a team meeting with all Thessen Virtual Lab experts to discuss a research agenda.

## Team Members

The following experts will contribute their perspectives:

1. **Architect** (Team Lead) - Systems design, research coordination
2. **Bioinformaticist** - Genomics, computational biology
3. **Scientific Critic** - Ensures rigor, identifies weaknesses
4. **Data Curator** - Data management, FAIR principles
5. **Clinical Informaticist** - Health informatics, clinical relevance
6. **Tick Ecologist** - Vector biology, ecology
7. **Public Health Researcher** - Epidemiology, policy

## Meeting Structure

When running a team meeting:

### 1. Opening (Architect)
The Architect opens by:
- Summarizing the agenda
- Identifying key questions to address
- Outlining how each expert's perspective is relevant

### 2. Expert Input (All Members)
Each expert provides their perspective:
- Insights from their domain
- Concerns or challenges they foresee
- Suggestions and recommendations
- Questions for other team members

### 3. Discussion
Facilitate dialogue between experts:
- Address points of disagreement
- Build on complementary ideas
- Identify gaps in knowledge

### 4. Synthesis (Architect)
The Architect concludes by:
- Summarizing key points from each expert
- Identifying areas of consensus
- Noting remaining questions
- Proposing next steps

## How to Use

To run a team meeting, provide:

1. **Agenda**: The topic or research question to discuss
2. **Questions** (optional): Specific questions to address
3. **Context** (optional): Background information or prior work

Example prompt:
```
/team-meeting

Agenda: Develop a tick surveillance strategy for suburban parks

Questions:
1. What sampling methods should we use?
2. How do we integrate ecological and clinical data?
3. What are the key public health messages?
```

## Output Format

The meeting output will include:
- Summary of each expert's key points
- Areas of consensus and disagreement
- Recommended next steps
- Open questions for further investigation
